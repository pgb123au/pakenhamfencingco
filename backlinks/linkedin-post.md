## Version 2 — Operator voice

A quick PSA for anyone putting in or replacing a pool in Pakenham this season.

Pool fencing is the job people most often get wrong, and it's the one Cardinia Shire enforces hardest. Under the Victorian barrier regulations and AS 1926.1 your barrier needs an effective height of at least 1.2 m, a gate that's self-closing AND self-latching and swings outward, the latch at the right height, and — the one everyone forgets — a clear non-climbable zone. That 900 mm NCZ means no pot plants, no pump housing, no BBQ, nothing a small child could use as a step anywhere near the fence. In Victoria the barrier also has to be registered with council and inspected.

Out on the new estate lots — Lakeside, the latest Officer releases — there's the added factor of wind. Exposed blocks with no mature trees put real load on glass and aluminium panels, so post spacing and footing depth matter more than people expect on reactive clay.

None of it is hard when you do it daily. It's just unforgiving when you don't. If you're booking a pool over summer, sort the barrier at the same time so you pass inspection first go rather than fencing off the pool twice.
